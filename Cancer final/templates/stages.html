Lung Cancer Types & Stages

Understanding classification and progression of lung cancer

Types of Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is broadly classified into two major types based on how the cancer cells appear under a microscope:

  • Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) – The most common type (about 85% of cases). It grows and spreads more slowly compared to SCLC.
  • Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC) – A less common but more aggressive form that spreads rapidly.

Treatment options vary depending on the type, size, location, and spread of the tumor.

Stage I – Early Stage

Cancer is confined to the lung and has not spread to lymph nodes. Symptoms may be mild or absent. Early detection at this stage offers the best chance for successful treatment.

Stage II – Local Spread

Cancer has spread to nearby lymph nodes within the lung. Symptoms may include persistent cough, chest pain, and shortness of breath. Surgery combined with chemotherapy is often recommended.

Stage III – Regional Spread

Cancer has spread to lymph nodes in the center of the chest. Treatment may include a combination of surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy. This stage requires aggressive management.

Stage IV – Advanced Stage

Cancer has spread (metastasized) to distant organs such as the brain, liver, or bones. Treatment focuses on slowing progression and improving quality of life. Targeted therapy and immunotherapy are commonly used.

Importance of Early Detection

The earlier lung cancer is detected, the higher the survival rate. AI-based predictive systems help in preliminary risk assessment, encouraging individuals to seek medical evaluation at the right time.
